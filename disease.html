Diseases Caused by Improper Waste Management

Malaria

Malaria is a mosquito-borne disease that can be transmitted through stagnant water often found in waste dumping sites.

Dengue Fever

Dengue fever is also spread by mosquitoes that breed in stagnant water, making waste disposal crucial to prevent its spread.

Cholera

Cholera can be caused by consuming contaminated water, which can result from improper disposal of waste near water sources.

Respiratory Problems

Inhaling fumes from burning waste or exposure to hazardous chemicals in waste can lead to various respiratory problems.

For a healthier and cleaner environment, proper waste management is essential.
